# Types of Investment Accounts: Choosing the Right One

## Overview

Different account types offer different tax advantages. Choosing the right accounts for your goals can save you thousands in taxes.

## Tax-Advantaged Retirement Accounts

### Traditional 401(k)
**Employer-sponsored retirement plan**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+) |
| Tax on Contributions | Pre-tax (reduces taxable income) |
| Tax on Growth | Tax-deferred |
| Tax on Withdrawals | Taxed as ordinary income |
| Early Withdrawal | 10% penalty before 59Â½ |
| RMDs | Required at age 73 |

**Best for:** Employees with high current income who expect lower taxes in retirement.

### Roth 401(k)
**After-tax version of 401(k)**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+) |
| Tax on Contributions | After-tax (no deduction) |
| Tax on Growth | Tax-free |
| Tax on Withdrawals | Tax-free (if qualified) |
| Early Withdrawal | Contributions tax/penalty-free |
| RMDs | Required at 73 (can roll to Roth IRA to avoid) |

**Best for:** Those expecting higher taxes in retirement; younger workers.

### Traditional IRA
**Individual retirement account**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+) |
| Tax on Contributions | May be deductible |
| Tax on Growth | Tax-deferred |
| Tax on Withdrawals | Taxed as ordinary income |
| Income Limits | Deductibility phases out with employer plan |
| RMDs | Required at age 73 |

**Best for:** Those without employer retirement plans or with moderate income.

### Roth IRA
**Tax-free growth retirement account**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+) |
| Tax on Contributions | After-tax |
| Tax on Growth | Tax-free |
| Tax on Withdrawals | Tax-free (if qualified) |
| Income Limits | Cannot contribute directly above limits |
| RMDs | None during owner's lifetime |

**Income Limits (2024 - Single):**
- Full contribution: Under $146,000
- Partial: $146,000-$161,000
- None: Over $161,000

**Best for:** Young workers, those expecting higher future taxes, estate planning.

## Self-Employed Retirement Accounts

### SEP IRA
**Simplified Employee Pension**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | Up to 25% of compensation or $69,000 |
| Who Contributes | Employer only |
| Best For | Self-employed with high income |

### Solo 401(k)
**Individual 401(k) for self-employed**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | $69,000 ($76,500 if 50+) |
| Who Contributes | Both employer and employee contributions |
| Best For | Self-employed wanting maximum savings |

### SIMPLE IRA
**Savings Incentive Match Plan**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | $16,000 ($19,500 if 50+) |
| Best For | Small businesses with employees |

## Education Accounts

### 529 Plan
**College savings plan**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit | Varies by state ($300K+ lifetime) |
| Tax on Contributions | After-tax (may get state deduction) |
| Tax on Growth | Tax-free |
| Tax on Withdrawals | Tax-free for education expenses |
| Penalty | 10% + taxes if not used for education |

**Best for:** Saving for children's/grandchildren's education.

### Coverdell ESA
**Education Savings Account**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit | $2,000/year per beneficiary |
| Tax Treatment | Similar to 529 |
| Flexibility | K-12 and college expenses |
| Income Limits | Phases out at higher incomes |

## Health Savings Account (HSA)

**Triple tax advantage for healthcare**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit (2024) | $4,150 individual, $8,300 family |
| Eligibility | Must have High Deductible Health Plan |
| Tax on Contributions | Pre-tax |
| Tax on Growth | Tax-free |
| Tax on Withdrawals | Tax-free for medical expenses |

**The Triple Tax Advantage:**
1. Contributions reduce taxable income
2. Growth is tax-free
3. Withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free

**After 65:** Can withdraw for any purpose (taxed as income, no penalty).

**Best for:** Those with HDHP who can afford to pay medical expenses out of pocket.

## Taxable Brokerage Account

**Regular investment account with no tax advantages**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Contribution Limit | Unlimited |
| Tax on Contributions | After-tax |
| Tax on Dividends | Taxed annually |
| Tax on Capital Gains | Taxed when sold |
| Access | Anytime, no penalties |

**When to Use:**
- After maxing tax-advantaged accounts
- Money needed before retirement
- No income limits
- Want flexibility

**Tax-Efficient Strategies:**
- Hold index funds (low turnover)
- Use tax-loss harvesting
- Hold bonds in tax-advantaged accounts
- Keep investments for 1+ year (long-term rates)

## Account Priority Order

### Recommended Contribution Order:

1. **401(k) to employer match** (free money)
2. **HSA** (if eligible - triple tax advantage)
3. **Roth IRA** (if eligible)
4. **Max 401(k)** (remaining contribution room)
5. **Taxable brokerage** (after tax-advantaged is maxed)

### Alternative Order (High Income):

1. **401(k) to max** ($23,000)
2. **Backdoor Roth IRA** ($7,000)
3. **HSA** (if eligible)
4. **Mega Backdoor Roth** (if 401(k) allows)
5. **Taxable brokerage**

## Asset Location Strategy

**Which investments in which accounts:**

| Investment Type | Best Account | Reason |
|-----------------|--------------|--------|
| Bonds | Tax-deferred (401k, Traditional IRA) | Interest taxed as ordinary income |
| REITs | Tax-deferred | High dividends taxed as ordinary income |
| High-growth stocks | Roth IRA | Maximize tax-free growth |
| Index funds | Taxable | Tax-efficient, low turnover |
| International stocks | Taxable | Foreign tax credit |

## Comparing Account Types

| Account | Tax on In | Tax on Growth | Tax on Out | Limits |
|---------|-----------|---------------|------------|--------|
| Traditional 401(k) | Deductible | Deferred | Ordinary income | $23K |
| Roth 401(k) | After-tax | Tax-free | Tax-free | $23K |
| Traditional IRA | Maybe deductible | Deferred | Ordinary income | $7K |
| Roth IRA | After-tax | Tax-free | Tax-free | $7K |
| HSA | Deductible | Tax-free | Tax-free (medical) | $4.15K |
| 529 | After-tax | Tax-free | Tax-free (education) | High |
| Taxable | After-tax | Taxed annually | Capital gains | Unlimited |

## Key Takeaways

1. Max employer 401(k) match first (free money)
2. Roth accounts are powerful for tax-free growth
3. HSAs have the best tax benefits if you qualify
4. Use taxable accounts after maxing tax-advantaged
5. Consider asset location for tax efficiency
6. Your situation determines optimal account mix
