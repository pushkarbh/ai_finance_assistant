# Financial Glossary: Essential Terms for Investors

## A

**Asset Allocation:** The distribution of investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) based on goals and risk tolerance.

**APY (Annual Percentage Yield):** The real rate of return on an investment, accounting for compound interest.

**Ask Price:** The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security.

## B

**Bear Market:** A market condition where prices fall 20% or more from recent highs, typically accompanied by pessimism.

**Bid Price:** The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security.

**Blue Chip:** Large, well-established companies with stable earnings and dividend history.

**Bond:** A fixed-income investment where you lend money to an entity (government or corporation) in exchange for interest payments.

**Bull Market:** A market condition with rising prices and investor optimism.

## C

**Capital Gain:** Profit from selling an investment for more than you paid.

**Capital Loss:** Loss from selling an investment for less than you paid.

**Compound Interest:** Interest calculated on both the initial principal and accumulated interest.

**Cost Basis:** The original value of an investment for tax purposes.

**Coupon Rate:** The annual interest rate paid on a bond.

## D

**Diversification:** Spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk.

**Dividend:** A distribution of company profits to shareholders.

**Dividend Yield:** Annual dividend divided by stock price, expressed as a percentage.

**Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA):** Investing fixed amounts at regular intervals regardless of price.

**Duration:** A measure of a bond's sensitivity to interest rate changes.

## E

**Emerging Markets:** Developing economies with higher growth potential and risk (China, India, Brazil).

**Equity:** Ownership in a company; synonymous with stock.

**ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund):** A fund that trades on exchanges like stocks, typically tracking an index.

**Ex-Dividend Date:** The date after which new buyers don't receive the declared dividend.

**Expense Ratio:** Annual fee charged by a fund, expressed as a percentage of assets.

## F

**FDIC:** Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation; insures bank deposits up to $250,000.

**Fiduciary:** Someone legally required to act in your best interest.

**Fixed Income:** Investments that pay regular interest (bonds, CDs).

**401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement plan with tax advantages.

**Front-End Load:** Sales charge paid when buying a mutual fund.

## G

**Growth Stock:** Stock of a company expected to grow faster than average, usually pays little or no dividend.

**Glide Path:** Gradual shift in asset allocation over time, typically from aggressive to conservative.

## H

**High-Yield Bond:** Also called "junk bond"; lower credit quality but higher interest rate.

**Holding Period:** The length of time an investment is owned.

## I

**Index:** A measurement of market performance (S&P 500, Dow Jones).

**Index Fund:** A fund designed to match the performance of a market index.

**Inflation:** The rate at which prices increase over time, reducing purchasing power.

**IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Tax-advantaged retirement account for individuals.

## J

**Junk Bond:** See High-Yield Bond.

## L

**Large-Cap:** Companies with market capitalization over $10 billion.

**Liquidity:** How easily an investment can be converted to cash.

**Load:** Sales commission charged by some mutual funds.

**Long-Term Capital Gain:** Profit from selling an investment held over one year; taxed at lower rates.

## M

**Market Capitalization (Market Cap):** Total value of a company's outstanding shares (stock price Ã— shares).

**Maturity Date:** When a bond's principal is repaid to the investor.

**Mid-Cap:** Companies with market capitalization between $2-10 billion.

**Mutual Fund:** Investment fund that pools money from many investors to buy diversified securities.

## N

**NAV (Net Asset Value):** Per-share value of a mutual fund's holdings.

**Nasdaq:** Stock exchange known for technology companies.

**Net Worth:** Assets minus liabilities; your total financial value.

## O

**Ordinary Income:** Income taxed at regular income tax rates (wages, interest).

## P

**P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings):** Stock price divided by earnings per share; a valuation metric.

**Par Value:** Face value of a bond, typically $1,000.

**Passive Investing:** Strategy of buying and holding market index funds.

**Portfolio:** Collection of all your investments.

**Principal:** Original amount invested or borrowed.

## Q

**Qualified Dividend:** Dividend taxed at lower capital gains rates rather than ordinary income rates.

## R

**Rebalancing:** Adjusting portfolio back to target allocation.

**REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust):** Company that owns/operates real estate; must distribute 90% of income as dividends.

**Required Minimum Distribution (RMD):** Mandatory annual withdrawals from retirement accounts starting at age 73.

**Risk Tolerance:** Your ability and willingness to endure investment losses.

**Roth IRA:** Retirement account with after-tax contributions and tax-free withdrawals.

**RSU (Restricted Stock Unit):** Company stock granted as compensation, vesting over time.

## S

**S&P 500:** Index of 500 largest U.S. companies by market cap.

**SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission):** Federal agency regulating securities markets.

**Short-Term Capital Gain:** Profit from selling an investment held one year or less; taxed as ordinary income.

**Small-Cap:** Companies with market capitalization under $2 billion.

**Stock:** Ownership share in a company.

**Stock Split:** Division of existing shares into more shares at lower price.

## T

**Target-Date Fund:** Fund that automatically adjusts allocation based on target retirement year.

**Tax-Deferred:** Taxes postponed until withdrawal (traditional IRA, 401(k)).

**Tax-Loss Harvesting:** Selling losing investments to offset gains for tax purposes.

**TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities):** Government bonds that adjust for inflation.

**Total Return:** Investment gain including both price appreciation and dividends.

## V

**Value Stock:** Stock that appears undervalued based on fundamentals; often pays dividends.

**Vesting:** Process of earning ownership of employer-provided benefits (RSUs, 401(k) match).

**Volatility:** The degree of price fluctuation in an investment.

## W

**Wash Sale:** Selling a security at a loss and buying substantially identical security within 30 days; loss is disallowed for tax purposes.

## Y

**Yield:** Income return on an investment, typically expressed as annual percentage.

**Yield Curve:** Graph showing interest rates across different bond maturities.

**Yield to Maturity (YTM):** Total return expected on a bond if held until maturity.

## Numbers

**4% Rule:** Guideline suggesting you can withdraw 4% of retirement savings annually with low risk of running out.

**72, Rule of:** Divide 72 by interest rate to estimate years to double your money.

**529 Plan:** Tax-advantaged savings plan for education expenses.
